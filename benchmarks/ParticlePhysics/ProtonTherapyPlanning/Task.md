# Task: Intensity-Modulated Proton Therapy (IMPT) Dose Optimization

## 1. Physical and Medical Engineering Background
Proton therapy is currently one of the most advanced and expensive radiation therapy techniques in the world. While traditional X-rays continuously release energy as they penetrate the human body (causing extensive damage to healthy tissues along the path), high-energy proton beams possess a unique physical characteristic known as the "Bragg Peak" . Protons release very little energy in the initial part of their tissue penetration (the plateau region) and instantaneously release the vast majority of their energy upon reaching a specific depth, after which the dose drops sharply to zero.

**Real-world Engineering and Medical Challenges**:
To cover a three-dimensional tumor, modern proton accelerators use "Pencil Beam Scanning (PBS)" technology. By varying the magnetic field and the initial energy of the protons, the accelerator directs hundreds of tiny proton "spots" onto the 3D grid of the tumor.
1. **Target Coverage**: The Clinical Target Volume (CTV, the tumor) must receive an adequate and uniform Prescription Dose to completely eradicate cancer cells.
2. **Organ at Risk (OAR) Sparing**: Healthy organs that are extremely sensitive (e.g., the brainstem) are often located immediately adjacent to the tumor. If the radiation dose received by these organs exceeds their tolerance thresholds, irreversible medical accidents such as paralysis or blindness will occur.
3. **Machine Wear Cost (Monitor Units)**: To extend the lifespan of accelerators worth hundreds of millions of dollars and improve patient throughput, treatment plans should aim to achieve the goals using the lowest possible total proton beam current (sum of weights).

This task requires the AI Agent to select the projection coordinates of proton pencil beams and optimize their spot weights within a complex 3D anatomical structure, finding the optimal mathematical balance between "eradicating the tumor" and "sparing the brainstem."

## 2. Physical Model & Environment Setup
We simplify the patient's brain into a 3D coordinate system (units in **mm**). Proton beams enter uniformly from directly above (the skin surface at $z=0$) and travel downwards (the positive direction of the $Z$-axis).
* **Clinical Target Volume (CTV)**: A sphere centered at $(0, 0, 50)$ with a radius of **15 mm**.
* **Organ at Risk (OAR)**: A sphere centered at $(0, 20, 60)$ with a radius of **10 mm**. (Note: The OAR is extremely close to the CTV and highly susceptible to collateral damage).

**Agent Action**:
The Agent must output a list containing $N$ proton spots (maximum $N = 100$). Each spot $j$ is defined by 4 parameters: $(x_j, y_j, z_j, w_j)$.
* $(x_j, y_j, z_j)$: The 3D physical stopping coordinates of the proton beam's Bragg peak (highest energy point).
* $w_j$: The weight (intensity) of the proton beam, requiring $w_j \ge 0$.

## 3. Objective Function
The total dose $D(P)$ received by any point $P = (x, y, z)$ in space is the superposition of doses generated by all proton beams at that point:
$$D(P) = \sum_{j=1}^{N} w_j \cdot K(P, S_j)$$

Here, $K(P, S_j)$ is the dose kernel function generated at point $P$ by a single proton beam stopping at $S_j=(x_j,y_j,z_j)$, simulating the Bragg peak phenomenon:
$$K(P, S_j) = \exp\left(-\frac{(x-x_j)^2 + (y-y_j)^2}{2\sigma_{xy}^2}\right) \cdot B(z, z_j)$$

The axial depth deposition $B(z, z_j)$ is calculated as follows:
* If $z \le z_j$ (during the proton's travel and reaching the peak): $B(z, z_j) = 0.2 + 0.8 \cdot \exp\left(-\frac{(z-z_j)^2}{2\sigma_z^2}\right)$. The **0.2** represents the entrance plateau dose.
* If $z > z_j$ (after the proton stops): $B(z, z_j) = 0.0$.
Constants: Transverse scattering width $\sigma_{xy} = 5.0$, longitudinal Bragg peak width $\sigma_z = 3.0$.

**The final score $J(W)$ maximizes the following formula (i.e., minimizing various penalties)**:
$$J(W) = 100.0 - \left( P_{\text{CTV}} + \lambda_{\text{OAR}} P_{\text{OAR}} + \lambda_{w} P_{w} \right)$$
* **CTV Coverage Penalty**: $P_{\text{CTV}} = \frac{1}{V_{\text{CTV}}} \sum_{P \in \text{CTV}} (D(P) - D_{\text{rx}})^2$. Target prescription dose $D_{\text{rx}} = 60.0$ Gy.
* **OAR Overdose Penalty**: $P_{\text{OAR}} = \frac{1}{V_{\text{OAR}}} \sum_{P \in \text{OAR}} \max(0, D(P) - D_{\text{limit}})^2$. Brainstem max tolerance limit $D_{\text{limit}} = 20.0$ Gy.
* **Machine Wear Penalty**: $P_{w} = \sum w_j$ (encourages minimal beam usage).
* **Weights**: $\lambda_{\text{OAR}} = 10.0$ (protecting healthy organs is crucial), $\lambda_{w} = 0.05$.
